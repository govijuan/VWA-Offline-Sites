When the sabara heard this, he thought: "Will I be cheated and deprived from the service to Sri Nila-Madhava. In this way he tied up his son-in-law and had him like that at home.

 

The sabara tied up Vidyapati because he was afraid to lose his adorable Deity.

After some time, however, since his daughter asked for the brahmana's liberation so many times, he permitted Vidyapati to go.

 

Vidyapati's wife taking away the ropes that tied him.

 

Immediately the king Indradyumna was informed about the great discovery. In great ecstasy, the king, with a lot of soldiers and servants, went to the place to be able to take Sri Nila Madhava with him. Of the seeds that Vidyapati took with him on his way, little plants had started to grow, so following the little road, the king could get to the place where he should find Nila Madhava.

 

The king Indradyumna following the road of mustard plants

But arriving there, they did not find Him, they could not see the form of the Lord. Thinking that maybe the sabaras hided the Deity, the king attacked the village and arrested Visvasu

 

The king did not find the deity of Nila Madhava.
