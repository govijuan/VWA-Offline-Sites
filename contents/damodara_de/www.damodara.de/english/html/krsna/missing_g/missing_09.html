The mothers and fathers of Vrindavan Village became more and more fond of the cowherd boys.

Sometimes, for no reason at all, fathers would carry their son in their arms. They did this even though the boy was quite able to walk on his own. Sometimes they stopped their work and played with their sons without even being asked. And sometimes they sand lullabies while their sons lay asleep in bed.

Not one father nor one mother suspected that their own child was asleep in a cave under a magical spell.
