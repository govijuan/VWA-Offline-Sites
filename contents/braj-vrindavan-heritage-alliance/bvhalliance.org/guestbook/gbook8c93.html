Before the days of modern transportation (buses, trains, planes & automobiles) Keshi Ghat was the front entrance to Vrindaban. According to traditional India culture the front entrance is tastefully decorated with banana trees on either side, geometric designs adorn the ground in front of the gate, water pots, and so on. With the advent of the modern day petrochemical culture Keshi Ghat has now become the back door where the rubbish gets chucked. Topsy turvy Kali Yuga. Because the residents allowed this area of Vrindaban to become defiled in the first place, it is not surprising that someone else came along to defile it even further. So this is a wake-up call for us all. Jiva jago jiva jago Gauracandra bole, koto nidra jao maya pishachira kole.
