Vrindavan: Tourist Mecca: Don't Ruin it w/Bridge

By Raghu G

Many depressed countries from Africa to China (and even) Burma have come to embrace the privilege of having such National Treasures like those of their wild life parks or magnificent temples of yore. These sites have many competing commercial prospects, but their governments recognized the greater (economic) benefits gained in protecting the original charm of these places. It builds upon their national legacy while also tapping their unique economic rewards.

Vrindavan is the Disneyland of Vedic Times. Keshi Ghat is the Gateway to that Magic Kingdom used for generations in tales, poetry, paintings and photos of its vast wonder. It’s the veranda to Vrindavan’s charm. The bridge radically degrades this grandeur of Vrindavan’s distinctive mystique.
