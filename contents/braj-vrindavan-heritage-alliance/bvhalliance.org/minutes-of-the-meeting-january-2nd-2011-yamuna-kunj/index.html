January 3rd, 2011Minutes of the Meeting – January 2nd 2011 – Yamuna Kunj

Posted by admin in News
Share

Chairman
-Sri Kanika Prasad Goswami – Radha Damodhar Mandir

Participants
-Sri Madan Bihari Das Baba – Tathisthan
-Sri Praduman Goswami – Mirabai Mandir
-Sri Gopananda Bon Maharaj – World Vaishnava Association
-Sri Paramadvaiti Swami – Vrinda Kunja
-Sri Lokesh Dexit Prabhu – Journalist
-Sri Parta Sarthi Prabhu – Food for Life Vrindavan
-Sri Pratap Singh
-Sri Nirmal Dasa – Gopinath Gaudiya Math
-Sri Man Mohan Parik – Journalist
-Sri Radhika S
-Sri Gopal Goswami – Gopesvar Mahadev Mandir
-Sri Chandan Kumar Prabhu
-Sri Dabanal Dasa – Worl Vaishnava Association

-Luc Stevens
-Srimati Arcana Devi Dasi
-Srimati Gandharvika Devi Dasi – Vrinda Kunja

Meeting Summary
*Kanika Prasad Goswami – Radha Damodhar Mandir
-One of the biggest problems in Vrindavan is the problem of water. Water is the most important thing in our lives. We should discuss what to do in order to clean the waters of the Yamuna.

*Paramadvaiti Swami – Vrinda Kunja
-We can identify two major problems: one is that water is very dirty and we are constantly polluting it. Another is that 10 years from now in Vrindavan waters will be dried. On one side we are putting poison in the water and on the other it is going to finish. So, we must do something very serious as soon as possible.

*Madhu Mangal Sukla
-We must solve the problems with the monkeys, because due to them we can not care for the trees either. They destroy everything.

*Pradyumna Goswami – Mirabai Mandir
I have seen the ancient temples of Vrindavan, formerly the ashrams have large green areas. Each time anyone goes to build a new building in Vrindavan they must show a plan or a demo of what they will do.

*Paramadvaiti Swami – Vrinda Kunja
-Sewak Sharan made – many years ago – a master plan for Vrindavan, where Chatikara road whole is a green area completely protected. Green areas mean water protection. Vrindavan is in a similar situation as a little baby monkey who must be protected by the mother and the other monkeys. We ought to get even angry against things which are damaging Vrindavan, we must raise our voices … otherwise we can not achieve anything.
-This is the meeting 40th of the BVHAlliance. Before we have seen some developments, but we must be more functional. We must clearly identify what issues are incumbent upon us in order to help Vrindavan. If people do not come to these meetings, then we ask signatures to have more support and present them before the NGOs and the government.
-The most important of all is personal activism; different organizations should individually do their part, and together make more pressure. To do this we have some forms that allow people to subscribe and more actively participate.
-Many people are encouraged to help, but they do not know how to do it. That is why we have committees that allow people to help on specific topics.
-The Environmental Committee of the WVA started a program called “Helping Hands” that seeks to secure funding for solve economic problems of Vrindavan. Thus we can generate work for Brijvasis as well as cleaning specific areas and provide specific solutions.
-We must be activists in cleaning our land and taking care for our water.
-Our meetings are to report on progress, get materials, but the practical work depends on what each of us makes in his ashram.
-We do not need to build more buildings in Vrindavan, we need more trees instead.

*Lokesh prabhu – Journalist of New York Times
-To return to Vrindavan from the outside I realized that the problems of Vrindavan were generated by ourselves.
-We must take more initiative to provide solutions
-Unless we make an effort to turn our dreams into reality, then nothing will happen.
-We must have the desire and energy to change this into reality.

*Baba Madan Bihari Das – Tathisthan
-We must become stricter in our cause
-We must abolish all plastics in the Ashrams; in Tathiesthan they do not use any plastic.
-We must win the backing of the Mahantas of the 300 temples of Vrindavan to our cause. I personally am going to do it.
-The government is not looking at sites for reforestation, they are looking for more parking places, they do not want to restore or protect the places, and they just want to bring more people.
-The use of plastics is very dangerous for our lives. We must abstain completely from its use within the Ashrams
-We should go talk to the office of the 4 sampradayas, because we all somehow belong to it and they can get much support. They should help us. Our next meeting we should realize it there.

*Parthasarathi Prabhu – Food for Life Vrindavan
-We must do everything to warn people about what is happening. We must do all these problems something public.
-The government has its own way, they never consulted us what to do, they are trying to develop Vrindavan, but in a material way, not spiritual.
-The most important thing is that people are informed to bring as many people to our BVHAlliance’s meetings. They do not know what is happening and should know.

*Arcana Dasi
-We must be careful not to just talk and talk, but to get into action.
-I propose the idea of going to different groups of people and educate them on all these issues. For example, we assembled a group of riksheros, we share a good prasadam with them and later we introduce them to everything that is happening and the possible solutions. The same with widows, schools, etc.
-In the same way we must create awareness in the government, with the help of people representing each mandir, even if the government does not want to listen we should have the support of the people.
-Each Ashram should commit to eliminate plastic inside the temple and spread to all its devotees about the bad of the use of plastic.
-We must make standard posters for hanging. In Cloth.

*Lokesh Prabhu – Journalist of New York Times
-We must define what kind of letters we want to send to the government. We should identify a type of letter to the government and other letters to people.
-For the government and the bureaucratic system really pay attention to our requests we must seek the support of skilled and professional people in the area, otherwise they do not know who we are and what we do.
-We should perform two types of pressure: one is to get the support of all people and the other is to press the government with important people who can support our requests.

Agreements:
-We will write 3 types of cards to alert the community and the government of what is happening and each solution.
-Letter about to abolish the use of plastics in the ashrams is to be written by Baba Bihari Das Madan, will add the issue of banning the burning of plastic waste is highly polluting.
-Letter on control of the buildings in the city will be written by Parthasarathi prabhu
-Letter – request for a land for basic water purification is pending.
-Every time an ashram decide to be against plastic, it is committed to spread it within its own ashram. Put posters to be designed for it.
-Vrinda Kunja-takes over the design of the poster which will be printed on canvas.
-The BVHAlliance has three offices available for outreach and awareness. One in Brahma Kunda, one in Pandavali Kunja (in Keshighat) and in Yamuna Kunja.
-It will be distributed participation questionnaires to all ashrams and thus to establish a link to more people.
-Baba Madan Bihari Das and Kanika Prasad Goswami will go to the office of 4 sampradayas to link to our work and to schedule the next meeting there.
